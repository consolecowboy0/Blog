## Overview

Racing line optimization is the process of finding the path through a corner (or series of corners) that minimizes lap time. This involves balancing:

- **Entry speed** — how fast you can carry into the corner
- **Apex placement** — where you clip the inside of the turn
- **Exit speed** — how fast you accelerate out, which affects the entire following straight

## The Geometric Line vs. The Fast Line

The geometric shortest path (the arc with the largest possible radius) is not always the fastest line. The fast line typically **late-apexes**, sacrificing entry speed to maximize exit speed onto the following straight.

> "Slow in, fast out" is an oversimplification — but the principle that exit speed matters more than entry speed is well-supported by the physics.
