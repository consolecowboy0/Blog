How the 2026 F1 Overtake Mode & Boost Systems Work

The 2026 F1 regulations dramatically increase electrical power. The MGU-K jumps from 120kW to 350kW, meaning nearly half the car's ~750kW total comes from the electric motor. DRS is gone, replaced by active aerodynamics and a new energy-based overtaking system.

Cars switch between Z-Mode (maximum downforce in corners) and X-Mode (minimum drag on straights, with front and rear wing flaps opening automatically). On top of this, the Boost button lets drivers trigger maximum electrical deployment at will -- but it drains the Energy Store faster. When within ~1 second of the car ahead, Overtake Mode activates: the chasing car receives an extra +0.5 MJ of energy and the MGU-K ramp-down is delayed from 290 km/h to 337 km/h, giving a significant straight-line advantage.

Regenerative braking zones become critical -- heavy braking areas recharge the Energy Store via the MGU-K (harvesting up to 350kW), and drivers must balance aggressive deployment on straights with ensuring enough energy is harvested in braking zones. The leading car must manage their Energy Store carefully, as they get no Overtake Mode bonus to defend with.

Monza is the perfect test for this system -- its long straights reward aggressive Boost deployment, while the chicanes and Parabolica provide critical regen opportunities.
