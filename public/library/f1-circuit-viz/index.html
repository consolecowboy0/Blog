How the 2026 F1 Overtake & Boost Systems Work

The 2026 F1 regulations dramatically increase electrical power. The MGU-K jumps from 120kW to 350kW, meaning nearly half the car's power comes from the electric motor. This creates a new strategic layer: Manual Override Mode (MOM).

MOM replaces the old simple ERS deploy button. Drivers can choose when to unleash the full 350kW electrical boost on straights, but it drains the battery faster. The chasing car gets an advantage: when within ~1 second of the car ahead, they receive extra MOM allocation — similar to how DRS currently works, but with far more driver control over when and how to deploy the energy.

Regenerative braking zones become critical — heavy braking areas recharge the battery, and drivers must balance aggressive deployment on straights with ensuring enough energy is harvested in braking zones. The leading car must also manage their battery carefully, as they get a smaller MOM allocation to defend with.

In this simulation, watch how Car B (blue, chaser) can use MOM strategically to close the gap and attempt overtakes on straights, while both cars recharge in braking/cornering zones. Click ⚡ Activate MOM Boost to manually trigger the chaser's boost and see the energy trade-off in real time.
